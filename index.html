1. Biden Withdraws From 2024 Race

Joe Biden announced he is stepping down from the 2024 presidential race and endorsing Kamala Harris.

Summary:

Joe Biden has officially announced he will not be running for re-election in the 2024 presidential race. The announcement was made from Wilmington, Delaware, as he recovers from COVID-19. Biden endorsed Vice President Kamala Harris as his preferred successor, encouraging the Democratic Party to rally behind her. With Biden's exit, attention now turns to the Democratic National Convention set for August in Chicago, where the party will formalize its nominee.


I believe it is in the best interest of my party and the country for me to stand down.

- Joe Biden


Kamala Harris, having received the endorsement, is now intensifying efforts to secure the Democratic nomination. She has already raised $46.7 million from small-dollar donations in the first hours following Biden's announcement. Moreover, Biden's campaign assets, worth an estimated $96 million, are set to bolster her campaign. Harris declared she is "honored" by Biden’s support and is committed to winning the nomination.

The political landscape has been electrified by the news, with various Democrats, including West Virginia Senator Joe Manchin, considering joining the race. With over 3,900 delegates set to participate in the convention's nomination process, the road ahead promises to be complex and highly dynamic. Republican strategists have already seized on the news, framing it as an opportunity to critique Harris vigorously.
