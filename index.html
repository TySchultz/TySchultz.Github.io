1. Supreme Court to Hear Abortion Pill Case

The Supreme Court has agreed to hear a case regarding the legality and access to the abortion pill, mifepristone, with far-reaching implications for reproductive rights in the United States.

What:

  • The case involves a dispute over the FDA's approval and regulation of mifepristone, which is widely prescribed to terminate pregnancies.

  • The appeal seeks to reverse a lower court decision that would limit access to the drug through telemedicine and mail, potentially shortening the time period for its use in pregnancy from 10 weeks to 7 weeks.

  • If upheld, the restrictions would make mifepristone less accessible, affecting over half of all U.S. abortions that use medication abortion.

  • The case will have implications for the FDA's regulatory authority and the accessibility of medication abortions in the country.

  • The decision is expected to be made by summer, a crucial time with the 2024 presidential and congressional campaigns.

  • Importantly, the case does not involve revisiting the FDA's initial approval of mifepristone in 2000, but rather focuses on subsequent regulations surrounding its prescription and use.

  • The Supreme Court's ruling will undoubtedly have a profound impact on reproductive rights and the availability of medication abortion.

Why:

Reproductive rights are always a hot topic, but this Supreme Court case could have far-reaching implications for the accessibility of medication abortions, affecting the reproductive rights of millions of women. The ruling will also signal the direction in which the Supreme Court may lean on future reproductive rights cases.
