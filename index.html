1. Google's App Store Monopoly Ruled Illegal

A federal court jury has ruled that Google has an illegal monopoly in its Play Store, after a lawsuit by Fortnite publisher Epic Games.

What:

  • The jury unanimously found that Google's Android app store and the Google Play Billing service constitute an unlawful monopoly.

  • The ruling could lead to changes in Google's Play Store rules, allowing other companies to offer competing app stores and making it easier for developers to avoid the cut Google takes from inapp purchases.

  • Epic Games, the maker of Fortnite, filed the lawsuit three years ago, alleging that Google has been abusing its power to protect the Play Store, which makes billions of dollars annually, while Google collects a commission ranging from 15% to 30% on digital transactions in apps.

Why:

This ruling could potentially disrupt the app store market, giving developers more power over app distribution and revenue. It may also result in more competition and lower prices for consumers. The outcome may also impact other big tech companies and could lead to changes in app store rules and commissions.
