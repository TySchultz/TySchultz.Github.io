1. Trump's First Rally Since Assassination Attempt

Donald Trump held his first campaign rally in Grand Rapids, Michigan, after surviving an assassination attempt. The event saw heavy attendance and focused on political opponents.

Summary:

Donald Trump hosted his first campaign rally since the July 13 assassination attempt in Butler, Pennsylvania, which saw the former president sustaining a 2 cm wide bullet wound. The rally drew 12,000 people at the Van Andel Arena in Grand Rapids, Michigan—a key swing state. Trump and his running mate, Ohio Senator J.D. Vance, delivered remarks criticizing Vice President Kamala Harris and the current administration's policies. They also commemorated Corey Comparatore, a firefighter who lost his life during the attempt on Trump's life.


They keep saying he's a threat to democracy. I'm saying, ‘What the hell did I do to democracy? Last week I took a bullet for democracy.

- Donald Trump


Updated health information following the shooting was provided by former White House physician Ronny Jackson. The atmosphere at the rally was reported as cheerful and patriotic, marked by a strong police presence due to heightened security concerns following the assassination attempt. Trump's speech encompassed calls for justice, a critique of political adversaries, and appreciations for democracy, despite the recent attempt on his life.

Amid the heightened security and the patriotic fervor, Trump and Vance took the opportunity to address their supporters, ensuring them of Trump's recovery and resilience. The rally served as a platform to not only critique the current administration but also to highlight the strength and dedication of their own campaign. With poignant moments of remembrance for Corey Comparatore alongside fiery political rhetoric, the event aimed to energize their base and reaffirm their commitment to their political objectives in the face of adversity.
