1. Republican National Convention 2024 Highlights

Key events and speeches from the Republican National Convention, including Texas Governor Greg Abbott's call for border security, JD Vance's VP nomination, and Peter Navarro's speech after a prison stint.

Summary:

During the Republican National Convention on July 17 in Milwaukee, Texas Governor Greg Abbott received a warm welcome from the Texas delegation while emphasizing the need for a president to secure the border, directly criticizing the Biden administration. Abbott's remarks were echoed by other speakers at the convention with similar sentiments about the current administration’s handling of border security issues.


America needs a president that will secure our border.

- Greg Abbott


In a significant move, Senator JD Vance was chosen by Donald Trump as his vice presidential running mate. Vance, originally a vocal critic of Trump in 2016, has since transformed into a devoted supporter of Trump’s America First agenda. His speech at the convention highlighted themes of party unity and his own humble beginnings, resonating with working-class Americans. Vance's selection was praised by several party members, including former White House official Mike Davis.

Former Trump trade adviser Peter Navarro, who served four months in prison for contempt of Congress, also spoke at the convention. Navarro drew parallels between his legal struggles and Trump's ongoing challenges. His speech, while meant to bolster unity among party members, also touched on contentious topics surrounding recent political events. Later this week, Trump will formally accept his GOP presidential nomination following Vance’s address.
