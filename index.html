1. Hollywood Labor Strikes

Writers and actors went on strike, leading to a halt in Hollywood productions over AI concerns.

What:

  • Writers and actors went on strike after their contracts with the Alliance of Motion Picture and Television Producers (AMPTP) expired.

  • The strikes were the first time both unions had gone on strike at the same time since 1960.

  • The contracts demanded protections against AI replacing writers and actors at their jobs.

  • The new contract guarantees that studios will not use A.I. tools to create digital replicas of performers without payment or approval.

  • SAGAFTRA members voted to ratify the new threeyear contract with Hollywood studios, marking the end of the 118day strike.

  • The new agreement raises minimum wage rates and includes protections around the use of artificial intelligence in entertainment industry.

  • AI protections included in the contract were a point of contention among members.

Why:

The Hollywood labor strikes are important because they highlight the ongoing debate and concerns about the use of artificial intelligence in creative industries, posing ethical and job security questions. It impacts the future of entertainment industry labor negotiations and the protection of human jobs from being replaced by AI.
